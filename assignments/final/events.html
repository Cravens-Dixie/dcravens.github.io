Ride the longest off-pavement route in the world.

The Great Divide Mountain Bike Route (GDMBR) is Adventure Cycling's premier off-pavement cycling route, crisscrossing the Continental Divide north to south. This route is defined by the word "remote." Its remoteness equates with spectacular terrain and scenery. The entire route is basically dirt-road and mountain-pass riding every day. In total, it has over 200,000 feet of elevation gain. Roughly 80% of it is composed of county, Forest Service (USFS), and Bureau of Land Management (BLM) dirt and gravel roads. The remainder is made up of four-wheel-drive tracks and singletrack trails (10%), and paved roads (10%). See Adventure Cycling's website for all the details, including maps of the full route.

Ride it all or join us for a 542 mile trek starting in Roosville, MT and ending in Polaris, MT. We plan to start our adventure July 25, 2018. Riding with a group is a party on wheels. Past group riders have commented that The Mountain Spoke group helped them not only complete the ride, but made it the most enjoyable adventure they had experienced in years. Our own bike techs and experts will be a part of the group and available to help with bike troubles on the trail.
