Springfield Breaks Record

Solar Eclipse! Businesses statewide are preparing for the massive influx of visitors that the once-in-a-lifetime solar eclipse will bring to Tennessee this month. With Tennessee tourism officials estimating as many as 1.4 million visitors coming to see the eclipse, up to 200,000 will travel to Franklin to spend nearly two-and-a-half minutes in the path of the eclipse on Aug. 21. The 2,700 hotel rooms in Montgomery County are nearly booked, homeowners are renting out houses and rooms and campsites are entirely sold out. Local venues – a vineyard and a distillery – are hosting viewing parties that feature eclipse-themed brews as collectors’ items and food trucks are prepping menus. The total eclipse band stretches through Middle and East Tennessee. Clarksville, Portland, Hendersonville, Nashville, Gallatin, Franklin and Cleveland each reside in its path, providing an economic boost to those cities and small towns as well as to the larger cities just beyond the band – Knoxville and Chattanooga – that benefit from proximity.
